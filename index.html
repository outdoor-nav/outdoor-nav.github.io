Reasoned Explorer: Reasoning about the unseen for efficient outdoor object navigation.

Abstract

Robots are envisioned to coexist with humans in various settings: indoors, outdoors, and even in unmapped terrains. Recent advancements in Object Goal Navigation (OGN) have predominantly focused on indoor navigation, leveraging spatial and semantic cues. However, these cues often don't generalize well to outdoor settings.

Shifting focus to outdoor environments presents a set of unique challenges. Unlike structured indoor layouts, outdoor terrains lack clear spatial boundaries and are characterized by inherent semantic ambiguities. Despite these complexities, humans exhibit remarkable navigation skills in these settings, primarily because of our ability to reason about the unseen.

In this context, we introduce OUTDOOR, a novel task aimed at addressing these challenges. Our approach utilizes Large Language Models (LLMs) to accurately predict potential future scenarios, enabling efficient navigation. Furthermore, we propose a new success metric, specifically tailored to assess performance in intricate outdoor terrains. Our evaluations, conducted on simulated drones and physical quadrupeds, demonstrate impressive results in diverse outdoor scenarios. Crucially, our agent operates without any pre-mapping, and our methodology consistently outperforms conventional LLM-based strategies.

Related Links

There's a lot of excellent work that was introduced around the same time as ours.

Progressive Encoding for Neural Optimization introduces an idea similar to our windowed position encoding for coarse-to-fine optimization.

D-NeRF and NR-NeRF both use deformation fields to model non-rigid scenes.

Some works model videos with a NeRF by directly modulating the density, such as Video-NeRF, NSFF, and DyNeRF

There are probably many more by the time you are reading this. Check out Frank Dellart's survey on recent NeRF papers, and Yen-Chen Lin's curated list of NeRF papers.
